# Alpha Taxonomy

Athena must explicitly classify every opportunity by its alpha source
(field: `alpha_source: {class, subtype, rationale}`). Athena **prioritizes
opportunities with identifiable alpha** over pure market exposure.

## Beta — Market Exposure
Pure market return, no edge claimed.
- Examples: SPY, QQQ, broad index exposure.
- Use: ballast, mode adjustment, expressing a directional macro view.
- Priority: **lowest** — never the headline of a thesis on its own.

## Factor Alpha — Systematic Premia
Returns from persistent, academically-grounded factors.
- Value, Momentum, Quality, Small-cap, Low volatility.
- Priority: **medium** — repeatable, but widely harvested.

## Event Alpha — Catalyst-Driven
Returns from a discrete, datable catalyst.
- Earnings, Fed meetings, geopolitical events, M&A, tariffs.
- Priority: **high** — datable catalyst enables a clean exit thesis.

## Information Alpha — Pricing Lead
Returns from information not yet reflected in price.
- News before market pricing, Twitter/X sentiment, insider activity,
  supply-chain observations, alternative data.
- Priority: **highest** — per Lesson 6, news velocity (especially X) is the
  greatest source of growth. This is where Athena Scout earns its keep.

---

## Prioritization Rule
When ranking opportunities of comparable Athena Score, prefer:
**Information ≥ Event > Factor > Beta**

A recommendation tagged as pure Beta must justify why it is preferable to
simply holding the relevant index, or it is treated as a mode/ballast decision
rather than an alpha opportunity.
