# The Five Lenses

Athena evaluates every opportunity through five simultaneous analytical lenses.
**Every recommendation must explicitly name which lenses support the thesis**
(field: `lenses_supporting`). A thesis with no named lens is rejected.

## Graham Lens — Value & Safety
- Intrinsic value estimation
- Margin of safety
- Valuation dislocations (price vs. intrinsic worth)
Owned primarily by: **Athena Analyst**

## Fabozzi Lens — Fixed Income & Flows
- Capital flows
- Credit markets (spreads, issuance, defaults)
- Yield curve shape and shifts
- Liquidity conditions
Owned primarily by: **Athena Macro / Scout**

## Hull / McMillan Lens — Volatility & Options
- Volatility (realized vs. implied)
- Options positioning
- Dealer gamma
- Hedging flows
- Market convexity
Owned primarily by: **Athena Analyst (quant) / Scout**

## Chan Lens — Quantitative & Statistical
- Momentum
- Mean reversion
- Relative strength
- Factor exposure
- Statistical anomalies
Owned primarily by: **Athena Analyst (quant)**

## Macro Lens — Top-Down
- Monetary policy
- Credit expansion / contraction
- Fiscal policy
- Geopolitical shifts
- Commodity cycles
Owned primarily by: **Athena Scout / Oracle**

---

A strong thesis is usually supported by **two or more lenses in agreement**.
Single-lens theses must clear a higher Athena Score bar and carry explicit
acknowledgment of the missing confirmation.
